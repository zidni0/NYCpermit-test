## What the official manual says

- The written test has 20 questions.
- You need at least 14 correct.
- You must get at least 2 of the 4 road-sign questions correct.
- The practice quizzes say the real DMV test can be different, but they clearly mirror the same rule patterns from the manual.

## Main question patterns

- The quizzes heavily reuse rule recognition, not hard calculations.
- Most questions are multiple choice with one clearly safe/legal answer and three tempting but unsafe answers.
- The biggest repeated categories are signs, right-of-way, alcohol/drugs, special conditions, and parking rules.
- Wrong answers often use these traps: `speed up`, `assume it is safe`, `coffee fixes alcohol`, `stop only if necessary when the law says full stop`, or `do what feels convenient instead of what is legal`.
- The mixed all-chapters quiz pulls many questions almost word-for-word from the chapter quizzes.

## Highest-value topics to memorize

- Sign shapes and colors.
- Stop, yield, flashing red, flashing yellow, green arrow plus red light.
- Right-of-way at intersections, especially left turns.
- Turn signal distance: at least 100 feet before turning or changing lanes.
- Emergency vehicles and the Move Over Law.
- Passing rules, including when passing is forbidden.
- School bus red lights.
- No parking vs no standing vs no stopping.
- Disabled parking rules.
- Alcohol and drug effects, BAC rules, chemical test refusal, and the fact that only time lowers BAC.
- Railroad crossing rules.
- Night driving and fog driving.
- Skid, tire blowout, and soft shoulder recovery.
- Pedestrian, bicyclist, and blind pedestrian right-of-way rules.

## Most likely question types and answers

- `Rectangular sign` = regulation sign such as speed limit.
- `Diamond-shaped sign` = warning / road hazard sign.
- `Destination sign colors` = green with white letters.
- `Warning sign colors` = yellow with black letters or symbols.
- `Yield sign` = slow down and be prepared to stop if necessary.
- `Flashing red light` = full stop, then go when safe.
- `Flashing yellow light` = proceed with caution.
- `Green arrow with red light` = go only in the direction of the green arrow.
- `Stop sign with crosswalk but no stop line` = stop before the crosswalk.
- `Police officer vs sign or signal` = obey the police officer first.
- `Left turn with oncoming traffic` = yield and wait, often in the intersection with wheels straight.
- `Two vehicles enter intersection at same time` = vehicle on the left yields to vehicle on the right.
- `Already in a traffic circle` = you have the right-of-way.
- `Enter roadway from private road` = yield to pedestrians and road traffic.
- `Right turn position` = close to the right side of the street.
- `Signal before turn` = at least 100 feet ahead.
- `Hand signal arm up` = right turn.
- `Hand signal arm down` = stop.
- `Pass in same direction` = usually on the left.
- `Pass on the right` = allowed when the other vehicle is waiting to turn left, or where otherwise legally permitted.
- `Passing always forbidden` = when a vehicle is stopped for a pedestrian in a crosswalk.
- `Being passed` = slow down slightly and stay in your lane.
- `School bus red lights flashing` = do not pass while red lights are flashing.
- `Single solid white line` = may cross only when traffic conditions require or to turn as legally allowed; quiz wording emphasizes not casually crossing.
- `Double solid yellow line` = do not cross to pass; quiz answer allows driveway turns only in some contexts, but for the provided question the correct choice is turning into a driveway.
- `Broken line crossing` = not if doing so would interfere with traffic.
- `No parking` = may stop temporarily to load or unload passengers or merchandise.
- `No standing` = may stop temporarily only to pick up or discharge passengers.
- `No stopping` = stop only to obey traffic, signals, police, or avoid conflict.
- `Never park` = in a crosswalk.
- `Hydrant distance` = not within 15 feet.
- `Disabled parking` = only with proper permit/plates and only when transporting the disabled person tied to the permit/registration.
- `Before leaving parallel parking space` = turn your head and check traffic, not just mirrors.
- `After parallel parking on level street` = straighten wheels and leave room between cars.
- `Get out on traffic side` = only when you will not interfere with traffic.
- `Safe speed` = depends on weather and road conditions.
- `Seat belts` = passengers and driver whenever in the car.
- `Work zone sign` = orange diamond with black letters.
- `Work zones` = rear-end collisions are most common.
- `Lane closed ahead` = merge when safe.
- `Road rage response` = stay polite, avoid eye contact, be ready to yield.
- `Drowsy driving` = stop at regular intervals for rest.
- `No posted NY highway speed limit` = 55 mph.
- `Alcohol plus other drugs` = can multiply/increase effects.
- `Only effective way to reduce BAC` = allow time for your body to remove alcohol.
- `Coffee or cold shower` = does not lower BAC.
- `BAC evidence of intoxication` = 0.08%.
- `BAC depends on` = amount consumed, body weight, and time between drinks.
- `BAC does not depend on` = physical fitness.
- `Chemical test refusal` = your license will be taken away / revoked or suspended.
- `Chemical test measures` = blood alcohol content.
- `12 oz beer removal time` = about 1 hour on average.
- `Alcohol affects` = reaction time, distance judgment, and recovery from glare.
- `Drinking and driving` = serious traffic safety problem.
- `Railroad crossing without signals` = slow down and be prepared to stop.
- `Cross tracks only when` = there is room for your whole vehicle on the other side.
- `Drive around lowered gate` = under no circumstances.
- `Vehicles that must always stop at railroad crossings` = school buses and passenger buses carrying passengers.
- `If train is close or fast` = wait until it has completely passed.
- `Heavy fog in daytime` = use low beams.
- `Night driving hardest because` = you can see less distance ahead.
- `Night driving` = drive within the range of your headlights.
- `Reduce oncoming headlight glare` = look to the lower right side of your lane / use road edge as guide.
- `Skid` = steer in the direction you want the front wheels to go.
- `Slippery road stopping` = brake in slow, steady strokes.
- `Tire blowout` = hold steering wheel firmly and ease off gas.
- `Soft shoulder recovery` = ease off gas and brake gently, then return carefully.
- `Missed expressway exit` = go to the next exit.
- `Expressway entrance lane` = reach proper speed before blending.
- `Expressway driving is different` = you must think faster and handle the vehicle more effectively.
- `Back out of driveway with children nearby` = get out and check behind the vehicle.
- `Pedestrian with no sidewalk` = walk facing oncoming traffic.
- `Blind pedestrian right-of-way` = with guide dog or white/metallic cane.
- `Bicyclist after dark` = front headlight and red taillight in the quiz wording.
- `Bicyclist on roadway` = rides on the right side.
- `Bicyclist difference from motorist` = bicycle insurance is not required.
- `Motorist near bicyclist` = exercise extreme caution.
- `Slow-moving vehicle emblem` = triangular orange sign.

## Topics that repeat the most across the provided material

- Alcohol and drug questions repeat very heavily.
- Sign identification repeats heavily, especially stop, yield, warning signs, and no-turn signs.
- Right-of-way and turn-position questions repeat heavily.
- Railroad crossing rules repeat in several places.
- Parking sign definitions repeat in both the parking chapter and the mixed quiz.
- Emergency vehicle and Move Over Law questions repeat in chapter and mixed quiz form.

## Trap patterns to watch for

- If one answer says `full stop` and another says `slow down`, choose carefully based on the sign or light.
- If one answer says `speed up`, it is almost always wrong.
- If one answer says `coffee`, `cold shower`, or `exercise` lowers BAC, it is wrong.
- If one answer gives the legally cautious choice and another gives the aggressive convenient choice, the cautious legal answer is usually correct.
- For parking questions, the test likes exact legal wording: `parking`, `standing`, and `stopping` are different.
- For sign questions, shape and color are often more important than text details.

## Best short memorization list

- `100 feet` for signals.
- `55 mph` default highway speed if not posted.
- `0.08%` intoxication.
- `1 hour` to remove about 12 oz beer on average.
- `15 feet` from a fire hydrant.
- `Orange diamond` = work zone / warning style.
- `Green + white` = destination signs.
- `Yellow + black` = warning signs.
- `Flashing red` = stop.
- `Flashing yellow` = caution.
- `Low beams in fog`.
- `Steer into the skid`.
- `Next exit if missed`.
- `Move over or slow down for emergency/hazard vehicles`.

## Bottom line

If a future task asks for likely test questions, answer explanations, flashcards, mock exams, or answer checking, focus first on:

- road signs
- right-of-way and turns
- alcohol/drugs and BAC
- parking sign meanings
- railroad crossings
- emergency vehicles
- night/fog/skid situations

These are the strongest repeated patterns across both the PDF manual and the quiz links.
